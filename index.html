Nujabes (1974-2010)

Master of hip hop and jazz from Japan

A Timeline of Nujabes' Musical Journey

  • February 7, 1974: Nujabes was born as Jun Seba (瀬葉淳) in the Nishi-Azabu district of Minato, Tokyo
  • April 23, 2003: The first compilation released by Nujabes' Hydeout Productions, "Hydeout Productions 1st Collection", was released, featuring Funky DL, Apani B, Substantial, Shing02, L-Universe, Pase Rock, Five Deez, and Cise Starr
  • August 21, 2003: Nujabes released first studio album "Metaphorical Music"
  • September 16, 2004: The soundtrack album "Samurai Champloo Music Record: Impression" was released by Nujabes with Force of Nature, Fat Jon, and MINMI
  • November 11, 2005: Nujabes released second studio album "Modal Soul"
  • November 11, 2007: Compilation album "Hydeout Productions 2nd Collection" was released, featuring numerous other hip hop and downtempo artists including Pase Rock, Uyama Hiroto, and Shing02
  • June 6, 2008: The compilation album "Modal Soul Classics" was released. It is a compilation of songs by other artists who have inspired Nujabes. All the songs on this album were compiled and mastered by Nujabes but were not composed by him
  • June 10, 2009: Nujabes released compilation album "Mellow Beats, Friends & Lovers" with Uyama Hiroto, Naoki Maeda, Dwele, Golden Boy, Sora, Takagi Masakatsu, Kuniyuki Takahashi and Rei Harakami
  • February 26, 2010: Nujabes died at the age of 36 in a traffic accident
  • November 11, 2010: Compilation album "Modal Soul Classics II", a tribute compilation album dedicated to Nujabes, was released. It was produced by various artists including Uyama Hiroto, Five Deez, Pase Rock, Calm, Zack Austin, Specifics, and clammbon
  • December 3, 2011: Nujabes' third studio album "Spiritual State" was released posthumously
  • December 9, 2015: Nujabes and Shing02's collaboration album "Luv(sic) Hexalogy" was released


Nujabes may not be physically with us anymore, but through his music, his legacy and lust for life lives on.

Daniel Hodgman, founder of hip hop culture website BonusCut, in a 2014 highlight of Nujabes' Modal Soul
